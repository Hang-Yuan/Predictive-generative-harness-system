## Overview

The **Focus Zone** is your weekly workbench—all in-progress work lives here. One file per week, clear boundaries. Raw thoughts become structured outputs here, decisions surface and get tracked, work-in-progress stays until ready to move to the library.

The Focus Zone runs on a **seven-day cycle**: week start you dictate or enter thoughts, AI breaks them into tasks, you push forward during the week, AI tracks progress, weekend you review, archive, and prepare the next round.

---

## Week File Structure

Each `_本周.md` contains **four blocks**:

### 1. Raw Notes
Your unprocessed input—ideas, questions, half-formed plans. AI doesn't edit this block.

### 2. Task List
AI-generated from raw notes, or appended during progress. Format: `- [ ] [Project] Task title — context/subtasks`

### 3. Progress Records
Daily work journal with project index. Organized by date sections (`### DayOfWeek YYYY-MM-DD`).

### 4. Weekly Output (written at archival)
Summary of actual outputs this week, written once before archival.

---

## Three-Phase Weekly Workflow

### Phase 1: Week Start (Sun/Mon)
Goal: Surface intent, break down tasks, confirm plan.

### Phase 2: Mid-Week (Tue-Thu)
Goal: Maintain rhythm, capture new work, unblock.

### Phase 3: Weekend Archive (Thu evening-Sun)
Goal: Confirm outputs, decide what stays/goes, prepare next week.

---

## Archive Rules

### Goes to Archive
- Completed work, closed tasks (marked complete >1 week), historical reference

### Stays in Focus Zone
- Work in progress, blocked items, current week file

### Archive Naming
Week close: rename `_本周.md` → `YYYY-Wnn.md`, move to `_归档/`, create new `_本周.md`.

Inheritance: Unfinished tasks from last week appear in new task list (marked [inherited]).
